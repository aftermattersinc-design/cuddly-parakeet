His story

A life well lived.

Brian "Buzz" Bee was born on 23 July 1949 in Hastings, Hawke's Bay - the younger of two boys, raised on the smell of summer grass and the sound of the sea. He got his nickname at school and it stuck for the rest of his life. Nobody called him Brian.

He spent thirty years farming the land he loved, and another decade doing the things he'd always promised himself he'd do when the farm was done - fishing, travelling slowly, sitting on the deck with a cup of tea watching the light change over the hills.

He was a man of few words and great presence. He showed love through action - through the firewood that appeared on your doorstep, the fence that got mended before you'd noticed it needed doing, the way he'd drive three hours without being asked just to be there.

He died on 13 August 2024, two weeks after being diagnosed with stomach cancer. He was 75. He was not ready. Neither were we.

This page is for him. Come back whenever you like. The lighthouse stays on.

His life

The journey he took.

1949
Born in Hastings, Hawke's Bay. The younger Bee boy.
1967
Left school at 17 to work on the family farm. Never looked back.
1974
Married Margaret Shaw at St Paul's, Greytown. She said yes twice - once at the proposal, once at the altar.
1989
Daughter Lola born. He cried for the first time anyone could remember.
2001
Sold the farm. Bought a fishing rod. Started sleeping in.
2019
First overseas trip - three weeks in Scotland. "Too much rain. Beautiful people."
2024
Diagnosed with stomach cancer 30 July. Died peacefully 13 August. Fourteen days.
